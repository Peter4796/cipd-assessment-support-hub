CIPD Assessment Planning Checklist

A step-by-step checklist to plan any CIPD assignment (Level 3, 5 or 7) with confidence. Tick each item before you submit.

Use this alongside your assessment brief. Every strong CIPD submission starts with understanding the brief and mapping the criteria, not with writing.

1 · Understand the brief

  • I have read the full brief and noted the submission deadline and word count.
  • I have listed every task and what it asks.
  • I have identified the command verbs (explain, analyse, evaluate, justify) for each task.
  • I can summarise each task in one plain-English sentence.

2 · Map the assessment criteria

  • I have listed every learning outcome and assessment criterion (AC).
  • I know which task addresses each criterion.
  • I have allocated a word budget to each task.

3 · Research & evidence

  • I have gathered credible sources (CIPD factsheets, textbooks, journals).
  • I have relevant workplace examples for application.
  • I am recording each source for my reference list as I go.

4 · Structure & draft

  • I have a focused introduction that sets scope and signposts.
  • My headings map to the tasks and criteria.
  • Each section moves from theory to application (not just description).
  • My conclusion draws points together and, if required, makes recommendations.

5 · Referencing

  • Every in-text citation has a matching reference-list entry.
  • My Harvard formatting is consistent throughout.
  • Online sources include an 'Accessed' date.

6 · Final review before submitting

  • I have mapped my draft against every criterion and confirmed each is met.
  • I am within the word count.
  • I have proofread for clarity, spelling and grammar.
  • I have addressed any previous tutor feedback (if a resubmission).
