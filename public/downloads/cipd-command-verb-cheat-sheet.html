CIPD Command Verb Cheat Sheet

The verb in the task tells you what kind of answer earns marks. Find your verb, check what it wants, and borrow an opening move.

Always answer the verb in your own current brief. If a task says evaluate and you explain, the content may be right and the answer still falls short, because markers assess against the verb that was set.

Explaining and describing

Explain

Say what something is AND why it matters or how it works. Two-part job: the what alone is not enough.

Opening move: "X means… It matters because…"

Describe

Give a clear, accurate account of what something involves. Completeness and clarity, not judgement.

Opening move: "X involves three main elements. First…"

Summarise

The key points only, briefly. Prove you can tell the essential from the detail.

Opening move: "In essence, X comes down to…"

Analysing and applying

Analyse

Break something into parts and examine how they relate. Look for causes, connections and effects, not just features.

Opening move: "Three factors drive X. The first, and most significant, is… because…"

Apply

Connect an idea to a real situation or organisation and show what it means there specifically.

Opening move: "In the case of [organisation], this means…"

Examine

Look closely and in detail, surfacing what is not obvious at first glance. Close to analyse.

Opening move: "Looking more closely at X, two tensions emerge…"

Compare (and contrast)

Set two or more things against each other on the same criteria: similarities, differences, and what follows.

Opening move: "X and Y share… but differ sharply on…"

Judging and arguing

Evaluate

Weigh strengths and weaknesses against criteria and reach a judgement. The judgement is compulsory.

Closing move: "On balance, X is most effective when… because…"

Assess

Judge the extent, value or importance of something. Like evaluate: evidence first, verdict required.

Closing move: "The evidence suggests X makes a significant difference where…"

Critically evaluate / critically analyse

Evaluate with the strongest opposing views weighed and answered, limits acknowledged, and a defended position. The standard at Level 7, and increasingly at Level 5.

Move: claim → evidence → counterview → response → judgement.

Justify

Give the case FOR a position or recommendation: reasons, evidence, and why alternatives lose.

Opening move: "X is the strongest option for three reasons…"

Discuss

Present more than one side of a question with evidence, then draw the threads together. Not an invitation to ramble.

Shape: "One reading of X is… A contrasting view holds… Taken together…"

Review

Survey the ground and appraise it: what is there, what is working, what deserves attention.

Opening move: "Current practice on X falls into three areas…"

Quick level guide

Level 3 briefs lean on explain, describe and summarise. Level 5 adds analyse, apply, evaluate and discuss, and expects judgement. Level 7 expects critical evaluation throughout: weighed counterviews and a defended position. Whatever the level, reread each finished answer asking one question: did I do what the verb asked?
