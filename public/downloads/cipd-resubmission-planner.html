Phase 1 · Decode the feedback

  • I have read every comment twice, slowly, without drafting anything yet.
  • I have listed each criterion or task the feedback names as not yet met.
  • I have translated every comment into a specific action in a specific section.
  • Anything I do not understand, I have asked my tutor about while support is available.

Phase 2 · Plan the revision

  • I know which sections stay, which change, and which need new analysis or evidence.
  • I have audited my word count and know what I will cut to make room.
  • I have checked the whole draft for each fault the feedback named, not just where it was flagged.
  • I have booked realistic work blocks in my calendar between now and my target date.

Phase 4 · Cool off, then final check

  • I left at least a day between finishing the revision and the final check.
  • I have re-checked the draft against every criterion in the brief, line by line.
  • Every point in the tutor feedback maps to a visible change in the draft.
  • I am within the word count and tolerance my centre allows.
  • Referencing is complete, consistent and matches my centre's guidance.
  • I am submitting before the deadline, not at the last minute.
