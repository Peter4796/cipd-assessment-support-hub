A clear structure keeps 5HR01 focused and makes it easy to mark. Organise your answer around the tasks and criteria in your brief, with headings that show where each point is addressed.

## A structure that works

1. A short introduction setting scope and context.
2. A section on employee voice and engagement, with analysis.
3. A section on conflict and dispute resolution.
4. A section on discipline, grievance and fair, lawful handling.
5. Employment law applied throughout, not bolted on at the end.
6. A brief conclusion, plus recommendations if asked, and a full Harvard reference list.

> Weave the law into each relevant section rather than isolating it. Applying law in context reads far better than a standalone legal summary.

## Plan your word count

Allocate a word budget to each task before writing, in proportion to its criteria. This keeps your answer balanced and stops you running short on the parts that carry the most marks.

## Theory and law to practice

In every section, move from the concept or legal point to how it applies in a real workplace, then to analysis. That rhythm keeps the whole answer applied and analytical.
