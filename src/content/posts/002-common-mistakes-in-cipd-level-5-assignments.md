CIPD Level 5, the Associate Diploma, is where many capable HR professionals get their first referral. The jump from Level 3 is real. Level 5 expects evidence-based practice, analysis and application, not just accurate description. Here are the mistakes we see most often, and how to fix them.

## 1. Describing when you should be analysing

This is the number one issue. Explaining what a model is earns few marks. Showing how it applies, comparing options, and weighing their suitability for a specific organisation is what Level 5 rewards. Whenever you make a point, ask 'so what?' and answer it.

## 2. Ignoring the organisational context

Level 5 answers should be grounded in a real or realistic workplace. Generic answers that could apply to any organisation feel thin. Anchor your points to context: sector, size, culture, current challenges.

## 3. Weak or missing evidence

Assertions need support. Use credible sources such as CIPD factsheets, academic texts and peer-reviewed journals, and cite them properly. 'Research shows...' without a citation is a red flag to an assessor.

> A good test: every key claim in a Level 5 answer should be traceable to either evidence, a model, or a clearly reasoned argument.

## 4. Referencing errors

Inconsistent Harvard referencing, missing in-text citations and mismatches between citations and the reference list are easy marks to lose. Build your reference list as you write, not at 2am the night before.

## 5. Not answering every assessment criterion

It's surprisingly common to miss a criterion entirely. Map your draft against each AC before submitting and confirm every one is evidenced.

## 6. Going over or under the word count

Wildly exceeding the limit suggests a lack of focus. Falling well short usually means criteria aren't fully addressed. Plan your word budget per task from the start.
