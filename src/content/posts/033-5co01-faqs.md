Here are answers to the questions learners ask most about 5CO01, Organisational performance and culture in practice. Always check the specifics against your own current brief and study centre guidance.

### Can I use my own organisation for 5CO01?

Yes, and it is usually the best approach. Using a real organisation you know well makes your analysis and application far stronger. If you cannot use your own, a well-researched organisation you understand works too.

### How many words is 5CO01?

Word counts vary by centre, so check your brief. Whatever the limit, allocate it across the tasks in proportion to their criteria, and stay within the tolerance your centre allows.

### Which models should I use?

Choose a focused set and apply them well. PESTLE or SWOT for the environment, and Schein or Handy for culture, are commonly used. Applying two or three thoroughly beats naming many.

### Why do people get referred on 5CO01?

Most often for describing rather than analysing, not applying points to a real organisation, or missing the link between organisational context and people practice. Our common mistakes guide covers how to avoid these.

> The theme across all of these: analyse and apply. 5CO01 rewards analysis linked to a real organisation, not description.
