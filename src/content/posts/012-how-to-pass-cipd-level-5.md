CIPD Level 5, the Associate Diploma, is a step up from Level 3 and the point where many learners face their first referral. Passing is very achievable with the right approach. Here is what consistently makes the difference.

## 1. Answer the criteria, not just the topic

Every mark at Level 5 is tied to a specific assessment criterion. Map each task to its criteria and make sure every one is clearly evidenced. Missing a criterion is the most common reason for a referral.

## 2. Show analysis, not just description

Level 5 rewards evidence-based analysis. Do not just explain a model or concept. Compare options, weigh their suitability for your organisation, and reach a justified conclusion. Whenever you make a point, ask yourself 'so what?' and answer it.

## 3. Apply everything to a real workplace

Generic answers feel thin. Ground your points in a real or realistic organisation, with its own context, so your analysis has something concrete to bite on.

## 4. Reference properly

Support your claims with credible sources and cite them correctly in Harvard style. Consistent referencing protects marks that are easy to lose.

> A pass at Level 5 usually comes down to three things: every criterion met, genuine analysis, and clear application to practice.

## 5. Use feedback if you are referred

A referral is not a failure. It is a clear list of what to improve. Address the exact criteria flagged rather than rewriting everything, and your resubmission should pass with confidence.
